Market Benchmark

No existing platform combines fractional co-ownership, community governance, data-driven location discovery, and a mobile super-app for digital nomads. This is simultaneously the greatest opportunity and the most dangerous risk.

Key Finding
Selina ($1.2B valuation) collapsed in 2024. Remote Year shut down December 2024. The market has a gaping hole in nomad-focused hospitality — and Slow Nomad is the only concept that combines ownership with community with infrastructure.
Pacaso
Closest Structural Analog
$300M+ equity + $1B debt. 1,000+ properties, 40 markets. $150M ARR. Each property in dedicated LLC; buyers purchase ⅛–½ shares ($200k–$945k). Just launched "Global Swap" in 2025 — directly mirrors our cross-booking. Average share price: $755k.
Gap we exploit
Pacaso targets wealthy second-home buyers, not nomads. Zero community features. No data layers. No nomad-specific infrastructure. It solves an ownership problem and ignores the lifestyle entirely.
NomadList (Pieter Levels)
Partnership Opportunity
$5.3M revenue in 2024. 100k+ members. 2,500+ cities ranked across 50+ data dimensions. 247+ real-life meetups annually. Tracks 383,000+ check-ins. The de-facto information layer for global nomads. One-person operation — Levels is distracted by AI projects.
Gap we exploit
NomadList is an information product, not a hospitality platform. It doesn't provide housing, facilitate ownership, or create structured communities. An API integration converts their informational audience into Slow Nomad residents.
Kindred
$148M raised · Feb 2026
300,000+ members, 150+ cities. Credit-based home-swapping — "give a night, get a night." Added 150,000 members in 2025 alone. Index Ventures + NEA backing. Plans "interconnected sub-communities."
Gap we exploit
No ownership — members swap existing homes. Not nomad-focused (90% primary residences). No workspace infrastructure. No data layers. It's peer-to-peer exchange, not portfolio management.
Outsite
Sole surviving coliving brand
~50 properties, 5,000+ members, $99/year membership. Profitable since Q3 2023. 4.9/5 ratings. Survived the brutal market correction that killed Selina, Common, Remote Year.
Gap we exploit
No ownership component — purely rental. Community building is their acknowledged weakness. Properties simultaneously listed on Booking.com, diluting community with transient guests. Basic tech stack.
Cabin DAO
Wound down 2025 — critical lesson
21+ coliving properties, 4,200 members, 355 "citizens" with NFT passports. Voted to wind down formal DAO structure in 2025. Coordination costs of bridging on-chain governance with physical property management proved unsustainable.
What this teaches Slow Nomad
Pure DAO governance fails for physical assets. Multi-day votes don't work when a pipe bursts. Slow Nomad's "DAO-like" governance is exactly that: community shapes standards during renovation, professional management operates within those standards. Community votes on what color the trash bags are. Management decides when to take out the trash.
NomadList Partnership

100,000+ digital nomads who check city rankings every month are Slow Nomad's primary audience. The question they ask NomadList — "where should I live?" — and the question we answer — "where should I own?" — are the same question at different depths of commitment.

🌍
Pieter Levels · NomadList
$5.3M ARR · Solo founder · 100k+ members · distracted by AI projects

Levels built the definitive data infrastructure for digital nomads — 2,500+ cities, 50+ dimensions, real-time check-ins. But NomadList is an information product. It doesn't house nomads, doesn't manage communities, doesn't facilitate ownership. His own words: "NomadList is becoming infrastructure, not a product." He's increasingly focused on Photo AI ($138k/month) and other AI ventures. The platform's community layer is Telegram-based and fragmented. The transition from "information tool" to "lifestyle platform" is a step he hasn't taken and likely won't take alone.

The Proposal
API integration that places Slow Nomad community data inside NomadList city pages. "X members currently in Tbilisi. Y slots available." Direct conversion path. In exchange: Pieter Levels gets a personal one-month-per-year slot across Slow Nomad properties — his own pin on the map, forever. He becomes the network's first high-profile Freediver. His audience stays engaged with NomadList because it now connects to real places to live, not just rankings.

📡
Foursquare Places API
Community pins · Local discovery · Gastronomy layer

Foursquare's Places API provides venue data — cafés, coworking spaces, restaurants, healthcare — that powers the interest layers on the Slow Nomad map. Members add community pins ("best espresso in Batumi," "reliable WiFi coworking near Viamonte") that layer on top of official venue data. This creates a proprietary community-sourced knowledge graph that competitors cannot replicate.
